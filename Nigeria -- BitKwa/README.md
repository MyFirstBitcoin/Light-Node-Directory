## üìñ About Us
BitKwa is a Bitcoin-focused non-profit organization based in Nigeria, established to advance financial inclusion, human rights, and economic empowerment through credible Bitcoin education and ecosystem development.

Operating at the intersection of education, technology, and grassroots community building, BitKwa equips individuals, institutions, and small businesses with the knowledge and tools required to engage with Bitcoin as open, permissionless monetary infrastructure.

BitKwa is intentionally positioned as a neutral, non-speculative, and education-first organization, committed to long-term capacity building rather than short-term adoption metrics.


**Mission**
To educate and empower Africans with credible Bitcoin knowledge and practical skills, enabling financial self-sovereignty, economic resilience, and participation in the global digital economy.

**Vision**
A future where African communities are financially sovereign, economically resilient, and globally relevant through the responsible use and development of Bitcoin.

**Core Values:**
Financial Self-Sovereignty: Promoting ownership, control, and personal responsibility
Open & Permissionless Systems: Supporting censorship-resistant financial infrastructure
Education Before Adoption: Prioritizing understanding over usage
Neutrality & Integrity: Non-political, non-partisan, and non-speculative approach
Community-Led Growth: Local empowerment with global alignment

**Strategic Objectives:**
Deliver globally credible Bitcoin education tailored to African realities
Build sustainable Bitcoin circular economies at the community level
Support youth, developers, and entrepreneurs entering the Bitcoin ecosystem
Serve as a trusted local partner for global Bitcoin organizations operating in Africa
Contribute African perspectives to global Bitcoin discourse

**Key Programs & Initiatives:**

**1. BitKwa Bitcoin Diploma**
A structured, cohort-based Bitcoin education program designed for learners aged 15‚Äì55.
Six-week curriculum covering Bitcoin fundamentals, self-custody, Lightning, security, and practical use cases
Delivered through online learning platforms with live virtual sessions
Designed to meet global education standards while addressing African socio-economic contexts
Graduates are prepared for further specialization, entrepreneurship, or professional roles in the Bitcoin industry

**2. The Bitcoin Hurdle**
A recurring physical Bitcoin meetup program based in Kwara State.
Monthly educational and community-building events
Targets students, professionals, entrepreneurs, and policymakers
Serves as BitKwa‚Äôs primary grassroots engagement channel
Emphasizes discussion, critical thinking, and practical Bitcoin literacy

**3. Bitcoin Circular Economy Program**
A community-based initiative to support Bitcoin usage among merchants and individuals.
Merchant education and onboarding
Peer-to-peer usage training
Emphasis on spend-and-receive, not speculation
Designed to strengthen local economic resilience

**4. Sats On The Road (West Africa)**
A cross-border Bitcoin outreach and partnership initiative across West Africa.
One-day meetups in multiple countries or 60-Day Tour
Knowledge exchange with local Bitcoin communities
Low-cost, high-impact grassroots engagement model
Strengthens regional collaboration and ecosystem alignment

**5. BitKwa Lab (Planned)**
A future-facing initiative focused on Bitcoin development and innovation.
Developer education and tooling
Research and experimentation on Bitcoin applications relevant to African markets
